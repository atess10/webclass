Japanese Macaque Social Behaviors!

-Japanese macaques practice “cultural transmission,” which means learned behaviors passed down from generation to generation. This includes washing food, seasoning food, catching fish, and the iconic bathing. (NEPC)

-Japanese Macaques enjoy sunbathing, travelling, and playfighting with snowballs. (NEPC)

-Japanese Macaques use coos to communicate, which reinforces social bonds and initiates grooming. (Animalia)

-These primates demonstrate altruistic social behaviors like sharing food and protecting one another. (ADW)
